Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about Lima-Plus and our services

How can farmers join the Lima-Plus program?

Farmers can join our program by contacting us directly through phone or email, or by visiting our local partner organizations in their communities. We conduct regular enrollment sessions and provide training to new members.

What crops does Lima-Plus support?

We support a wide range of crops including maize, beans, coffee, bananas, sorghum, millet, and various vegetables. Our climate-smart recommendations are tailored to specific crops and local growing conditions.

Is there a cost for farmers to use Lima-Plus services?

Our basic services including climate alerts and agricultural advice are free for smallholder farmers. Some advanced features and training programs may have nominal fees, but we ensure accessibility for all farmers regardless of income.

How does the climate alert system work?

Our climate alert system uses weather data and predictive models to send timely notifications to farmers via SMS and mobile app. Alerts include rainfall forecasts, extreme weather warnings, and seasonal climate outlooks tailored to specific locations.

Can organizations partner with Lima-Plus?

Absolutely! We welcome partnerships with NGOs, research institutions, government agencies, private companies, and international organizations. Please visit our Partners page or contact us to discuss collaboration opportunities.

What languages are supported?

Our platform and services are available in English, Luganda, and other major local languages spoken in Uganda. We're continuously working to expand language support to better serve all farming communities.
